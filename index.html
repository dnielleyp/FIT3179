Should You Study In Australia?

This visualisation explores Australia as a destination for students interested in pursuing their tertiary education abroad.
We focus on the members of the Group Of 8 which consists of Australia's highest ranked and leading research-intensive universities.
The Group of 8 educates more than 425,000 students, 160,000 of which are international students from around 200 countries.
One in three international students enrol in an institution of the Group of 8.


Host Countries for International Students

Australia is the second largest provider of education to international students, behind Luxembourg. It welcomes approximately 28% of students who've decided to study abroad. Students choose to study in Australia for countless reasons. The student visas are easily available, the universities are research-intensive and may offer internships or work opportunities to students. The cost of living in Australia is also lesser than other popular destinations such as Canada and the United Kingdom.

Top 15 International Students Origin Countries

The countries that send the most students to Australia are all located in Asia. China is the largest contributor to the international student population, with 37% or 1,600,000 international students originating from there. The second largest contributor is India, where nearly 20% or 886,000 students originate.

Australia is home different cultures, which allows international students to feel more at home in a foreign country. Many australian institutions have cultural events to celebrate and appreciate the different cultures their students are from.

Proportion of International Students

International students make up more than 20% of the student population in the members of Group of 8. Monash University houses 22,140 international students which makes up nearly 34% of their student population.

Fees Per Annum Of The Group of 8 Universities

University of Sydney has the highest annual fees out of the members of the Group of 8 ranging from $50,000 to $54,000. Fortunately, the Australian government has offered to provide scholarships to international students from across 55 countries. The institutions themselves also provide scholarship programs to international students.

Global Ranking Of The Group Of 8 Universities

On the right are the rankings of the members of the Group of 8 based on the QS World University Rankings. 7 of the 8 members of the Group of 8 are in the top 100 in the world and all the members are in the top 150 in the world.
